MERCURY

Mercury is the smallest planet in our Solar System and the closest to the Sun. Its surface is heavily cratered and resembles the Moon. Due to its proximity to the Sun, temperatures vary dramatically between day and night.
Mercury has a very thin exosphere composed of atoms blasted off its surface by solar wind and micrometeoroid impacts â€” not a thick atmosphere like Earth's. The surface shows cliffs (lobate scarps), plains, and large impact basins such as Caloris Basin.
Mercury has been visited by several spacecraft. Early flybys provided initial imagery and data; later orbiters mapped the surface and studied its magnetic field and exosphere.
